ORIGINAL EN

Global Climate Resilience Report

Executive Summary

Over the past decade, coastal cities have faced unprecedented challenges from rising sea levels and intensifying storms. This report examines adaptation strategies across twelve metropolitan regions and evaluates their long-term effectiveness.

Investment in resilient infrastructure has grown by 43% since 2020, yet funding gaps remain significant in developing economies. Public–private partnerships have emerged as a critical mechanism for bridging these gaps and accelerating deployment.

PAGE 2
Key Findings

Early-warning systems reduced storm-related casualties by an estimated 60% in regions where they were deployed. Coordinated evacuation protocols proved decisive in the most densely populated districts.

Nature-based solutions, such as restored wetlands and mangrove buffers, proved both cost-effective and durable. These interventions delivered co-benefits for biodiversity, fisheries, and local livelihoods.

The report concludes that resilience is most effective when embedded into routine urban planning rather than treated as an emergency response.

TRANSLATED ZH

全球气候韧性报告

执行摘要

在过去十年中，沿海城市面临着海平面上升和风暴加剧带来的前所未有的挑战。本报告研究了十二个大都市区域的适应策略，并评估了它们的长期有效性。

自 2020 年以来，对韧性基础设施的投资增长了 43%，但发展中经济体的资金缺口仍然巨大。公私合作已成为弥合这些缺口、加快项目落地的关键机制。

第 2 页
主要发现

在部署了预警系统的地区，与风暴相关的伤亡人数估计减少了 60%。在人口最密集的城区，协调一致的疏散方案起到了决定性作用。

基于自然的解决方案，例如恢复的湿地和红树林缓冲带，被证明既具成本效益又经久耐用。这些措施还为生物多样性、渔业和当地生计带来了协同效益。

报告的结论是：当韧性被纳入日常城市规划，而非仅作为应急响应时，其效果最为显著。
